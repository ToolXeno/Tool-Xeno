Contrast Checker

Our Contrast Checker ensures your color combinations meet accessibility standards by verifying that text and background colors have sufficient contrast for readability. Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) specify minimum contrast ratios to ensure content is readable by people with visual impairments, including color blindness and low vision. This tool automatically calculates contrast ratios and tells you whether your color combinations pass WCAG standards at different conformance levels.

Simply input your foreground and background colors, and the tool instantly calculates the contrast ratio, shows whether it passes AA and AAA standards for normal and large text, and provides recommendations if your colors fail accessibility requirements. It's essential for designers creating inclusive interfaces, developers implementing accessible websites, and anyone committed to ensuring their content is readable by all users. The tool helps you make informed color choices that balance aesthetics with accessibility.

Accessibility checking features:

  • Check color contrast for accessibility compliance
  • Calculate precise contrast ratios between colors
  • Verify WCAG 2.1 AA and AAA conformance levels
  • Test for normal text, large text, and UI components
  • Visual pass/fail indicators for quick assessment
  • Suggestions for improving contrast when colors fail
  • Live preview showing how text appears on backgrounds
  • Batch checking for testing multiple color combinations

Color Blindness Simulator

The Color Blindness Simulator shows how your designs appear to people with different types of color vision deficiency, ensuring your color choices work for all users. Approximately 8% of men and 0.5% of women have some form of color blindness, meaning millions of users may perceive colors differently than you intend. This tool simulates various types of color blindness so you can verify your designs remain clear and functional for everyone.

The simulator supports multiple color blindness types including protanopia (red-blind), deuteranopia (green-blind), tritanopia (blue-blind), and various other forms of color vision deficiency. You can test images, color schemes, or entire interfaces to see how they appear with different types of color blindness. This insight helps you make design decisions that don't rely solely on color to convey information, ensuring truly inclusive and accessible designs. It's essential for anyone serious about accessibility and universal design.

Color blindness simulation features:

  • Simulate different types of color blindness accurately
  • Support for protanopia, deuteranopia, tritanopia, and more
  • Test images, color schemes, and interface designs
  • Side-by-side comparison with normal color vision
  • Real-time simulation as you adjust colors
  • Detailed information about each color blindness type
  • Percentage sliders to simulate varying severity levels
  • Export simulated versions for documentation and review
