Frequently Asked Questions

The Andre Walker Hair Typing System is a classification system that categorizes hair into 4 main types (1-4) with subcategories (A, B, C). Type 1 is straight, Type 2 is wavy, Type 3 is curly, and Type 4 is coily/kinky. Each subtype describes the pattern's tightness and texture.
Our analysis uses the scientifically-backed Andre Walker system with comprehensive questions about your hair's physical properties. While individual variation exists, our 15-question assessment provides highly accurate results for most users.
Yes, hair type can change due to factors like hormonal changes (puberty, pregnancy, menopause), aging, chemical treatments, heat damage, or health conditions. We recommend retaking the analysis periodically or after significant life changes.
Hair porosity refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture. Low porosity hair resists moisture but holds it well once absorbed. High porosity hair absorbs quickly but loses moisture easily. Knowing your porosity helps you choose the right products and techniques.
Absolutely! Our analysis works for all hair lengths from buzz cuts to very long hair. The questions focus on your hair's inherent properties rather than length, making it equally accurate for men and women with any hairstyle.
Hair density refers to how many hair strands you have per square inch on your scalp. Low density means your scalp is easily visible, medium density shows some scalp when hair is moved, and high density means the scalp is barely visible even when hair is parted.
