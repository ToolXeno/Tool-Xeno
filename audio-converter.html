WAV (PCM) is the best for uncompressed, lossless audio quality — perfect for editing and archiving. For lossy compression with excellent quality-to-size ratio, MP3 at 256–320 kbps is the universal standard. OGG/Opus offers better quality than MP3 at the same bitrate and is ideal for streaming. FLAC provides lossless compression (half the size of WAV with identical quality).

Bitrate (kbps) determines audio compression quality — higher bitrate means better quality but larger file size. 128 kbps is acceptable for speech, 192–256 kbps for music, and 320 kbps for high-fidelity audio.

Sample rate (Hz) determines the range of frequencies captured. 44100 Hz (CD quality) covers the full range of human hearing. 48000 Hz is standard for video. Higher rates like 96000 Hz are used in professional recording.
File size changes depend on the format and quality settings. Converting to WAV (lossless) will increase file size. Converting to MP3, OGG, or WebM (lossy) will typically reduce file size — sometimes by 60–90% — depending on the bitrate selected. The conversion result panel shows the exact size comparison.
